- **Transport fork resolved (RESEARCH Q1 / Assumption A2):** Option A — a parent-side Queue-drainer over a `multiprocessing.Manager().Queue()` proxy — chosen and proven kill-safe. ## Decisions Made

- **Option A over Option B:** Queue-drainer keeps a single HTTP surface (`agent_client.post_analysis_progress`), inherits the tenacity retry funnel + `AgentApiError` hierarchy for free, and keeps `analyze_file` HTTP/pickle-free (only an `(int, int)` tuple crosses the pebble boundary). Option B (child-side sync `httpx.Client`) proved functional but was rejected — it duplicates the POST surface, couples the child to the bearer token, and forgoes retry reuse with no offsetting benefit now that A2 is settled.
- **Manager().Queue() proxy, not raw `multiprocessing.Queue`:** the manager process owns the queue, so a SIGKILL of the child cannot wedge an internal feeder lock; the parent can still `get`/`put` after the child dies.
- **Throttle lives in the bridge/drainer** (keyed on `monotonic()`, unconditional final flush), not in `analyze_file`.
