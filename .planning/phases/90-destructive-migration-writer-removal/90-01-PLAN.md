<objective>
PR-A of the readers-first D-09 sequence: convert **every** live `FileRecord.state` reader to derived sources
(the Phase 78 `stage_status.py` clause builders + the `cloud_job` sidecar) while `files.state` is still fully
present. No DDL, fully reversible, independently shippable and green. This is the mandatory prerequisite that
lets PR-B remove the writers and PR-C drop the column without breaking any dashboard count, the analyze
workspace, the proposal pending-set, the analyze re-drive backfill, the held-files ledger seed, or search.

It also **decouples the dedup-undo marker DELETE from `FileState`** so that when PR-B removes the
`previous_state` capture / dual-write / restore as a matched set, undo keeps working. The original PR-A
instruction to remove the `previous_state` capture in isolation was a **silent-break blocker**: `undo_resolve`
built its DELETE id-set from parsed `previous_state` and early-returned `if not restore_by_id`, so an id-only
payload skipped the marker DELETE entirely and undo no-op'd — while the hand-crafted undo tests (which still
contain `previous_state`) stayed green. This plan fixes the authority coupling and adds a real round-trip guard.

Purpose: RESEARCH Pitfall 1 falsified CONTEXT's "no reader consumes state" premise — ~11 live readers remain,
several read-coupled to the writers PR-B removes. Cutting readers first eliminates that coupling.
Output: `services/pipeline.py`, `routers/pipeline.py`, `services/dedup.py`, `services/search_queries.py`,
`routers/search.py`, `analyze_workspace.html` all reading derived sources; a FileState-independent dedup-undo;
extended reader tests + a dedup-undo round-trip regression.
