<objective>
N concurrently-dispatched Kueue clusters (MKUE-01, D-04): give each Kueue backend a DISTINCT kr8s
client built from its own `KubeConfig`, threaded through every `kube_staging` call, and RETIRE the
fragile post-construction `api.auth.token = token; await api._create_session()` hack in favor of
constructor-time auth from a synthesized in-memory kubeconfig dict (verified live against kr8s 0.20.15).
Both auth forms are supported: `kubeconfig`+`context` (parse the YAML content to a dict) and
`api_url`+`sa_token` (synthesize a minimal dict). The private `_api(kube)` / `build_job_manifest(file_id, kube)`
helpers already take a `KubeConfig`; only the public verbs still resolve it globally — lift that up.
Also fix the TWO companion single-non-local reductions that would otherwise 500 the moment a 2nd
non-local backend is configured: `active_compute_scratch_dir` (Pitfall 1, the `/pushed` callback) AND
its structurally-identical sibling `resolved_non_local_kind` in `backends.py` (the S3-upload-complete
callback `report_uploaded`, the `/pipeline/stats` poll `build_dashboard_context`, and the backfill route).

Purpose: prove cluster multiplicity — one control plane dispatching to N real clusters, each with
its own auth, `backend_id`-scoped reconcile, and per-cluster reachability probe.
Output: bucket-of-globals `active_kube` retired, per-backend kube client, token hack gone, all kube
callers threaded, controller startup probing every cluster, both single-non-local companion reductions
made N-Kueue-safe.

NOTE (D-05, deferred): do NOT fix the compute `agent_ref → Agent.id` heuristic (PROV-01). The
`active_compute_scratch_dir` and `resolved_non_local_kind` changes are DISTINCT reduction fixes, not
the deferred agent_ref work. The `>1`-compute-only fail-fast is retained (PROV-01 territory, unreachable
under D-05's ≤1-compute invariant).
</objective>
