<objective>
Collapse ALL FOUR push/upload callback CAS guards onto the `cloud_job` sidecar as the single CAS domain,
removing every `FileRecord.state` routing read (SC#1). Two deliverables:

1. **SC#2 (security):** `report_upload_failed` gains a CAS guard anchored on `cloud_job.status` (D-09) with a
   FULL no-op on miss (D-10), a `pg_advisory_xact_lock` on its attempt RMW (D-11), and the spill re-stamps to
   `status='awaiting'` instead of `FAILED` (D-03).

2. **SC#1 (routing-read removal / D-12):** `report_pushed` and `report_push_mismatch` — the two remaining
   guards still anchored on `FileRecord.state == PUSHING` — swap their CAS anchor onto
   `cloud_job.status == 'submitted'` (compute's single in-flight status, D-12). `report_push_mismatch`'s spill
   re-stamps to `status='awaiting'` in the same CAS (D-03).

Purpose: A late/duplicate/spoofed `/upload-failed`, `/pushed`, or `/mismatch` on an already-advanced file must
not clobber it (T-83-01 class), and two concurrent `/upload-failed` must not lose an attempt increment
(T-83-02). Per-endpoint kind-specific CAS (D-12) avoids the rejected universal predicate. After this phase all
four guards read/write the sidecar, so when Phase 90 drops `FileRecord.state` the guards keep guarding.

Output: rewritten `report_upload_failed`; anchor-swapped `report_pushed` and `report_push_mismatch`;
SC#2 CAS-no-op + D-11 concurrency + SC#1 /pushed and /mismatch clobber regression tests.
</objective>
