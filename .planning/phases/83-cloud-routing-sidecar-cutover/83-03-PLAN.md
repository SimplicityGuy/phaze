<objective>
Add the D-14 reaper: at both analyze-terminal seams (`put_analysis` and `report_analysis_failed`) delete
the file's `cloud_job` row when `status='awaiting'`. This bounds `ix_cloud_job_awaiting` growth — D-05's
conjunct (chosen over row deletion) means a locally-dispatched long file keeps its inert `awaiting` row
forever, and without a reaper the `*/5` drain tick scans a permanently growing dead set at 200K.

Purpose: The reaper is the real defense against the D-14 index-growth degradation (an EXPLAIN assertion is
not). It is independent of the helper and the drain cutover — pure additive DELETE at two existing seams.

Output: reaper DELETE at both seams; unit tests proving awaiting rows are reaped and SUCCEEDED/RUNNING
rows are left untouched.
</objective>
