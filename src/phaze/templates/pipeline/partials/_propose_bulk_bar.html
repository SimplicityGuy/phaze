{# phaze-a6hm.11: the bulk APPROVE / REJECT controls for the Propose workspace. WHAT THIS IS NOT. The workspace header carries a GENERATE ALL button, and these two are the easiest pair of controls in the app to confuse. They are different operations on different states and the confusion is not cosmetic -- one spends money and hours of litellm time, the other is the human-in-the-loop gate the whole product exists to provide: GENERATE ALL (header) -- CREATES proposals for files that have none. Acts on the corpus-wide PENDING-FILE set, is unaffected by tab/search/page, enqueues jobs. APPROVE / REJECT (here) -- DECIDES on proposals that already exist. Acts on EXACTLY the rows the operator ticked on this page. Writes status, enqueues nothing. So the wording is load-bearing and deliberately asymmetric with the header's: these buttons say "Approve"/"Reject", never "all"; the count they quote is the SELECTION, never a corpus total; and the hx-confirm names the operation, the number of rows, and the fact that the rows were selected. The header's confirm quotes propose_stats.pending (the corpus) for the mirror-image reason -- see propose_workspace.html. Neither may be reworded into the other's shape. WHY THIS POSTS TO /proposals/bulk RATHER THAN A NEW ENDPOINT. That route already owns bulk approve/reject: the phaze-uu17 from-state guard, the phaze-3st0 stale-id tolerance and the count that reports REAL transitions all live there, once. A propose-specific endpoint would be a third bulk path and would have to re-derive all three -- the bead forbids exactly that. Only the RESPONSE SHAPE differs between the two callers, and the router forks it on HX-Target, which is the established in-tree pattern for "same URL, two swap shapes" (see _render_stage, _v7_row_target). WHY THE VIEW STATE RIDES IN THE URL AND NOT IN HIDDEN INPUTS. The legacy bulk_actions.html spells six hidden s per form, twice -- twelve independent chances to drop a parameter, which is the precise failure mode view_state.py was built to end. Here the state is `propose_view.query()`: ONE call that emits the WHOLE state, so these controls state only what they change (nothing) and cannot omit what they do not mention. The endpoint re-parses it with the same ListViewState.from_request that GET /s/propose uses, so the post-action re-render lands on the same filter/search/sort/page by construction rather than by both sides remembering to agree. ID UNIQUENESS: this partial declares NO id at all. The checkboxes are addressed by a descendant selector rooted at the EXISTING container id, and the busy-gate uses `this`. Nothing here can duplicate an id because nothing here introduces one. CONCURRENCY: `hx-disabled-elt="this"` closes the double-click window client-side (the phaze-fa2p double-dispatch guard, same shape as GENERATE ALL). The real guarantee is server-side and does not depend on the browser: the UPDATE is constrained to `allowed_from=PENDING`, so a second, concurrent or replayed submission of the same ids matches zero rows and reports 0 -- the action is idempotent by construction rather than by locking (phaze-u28m TOCTOU). #}
{# Both buttons carry the SELECTION, not the page: hx-include serialises the ticked checkboxes inside this container. Unticked and disabled boxes are not serialised, so the request body is exactly the operator's selection -- acceptance criterion 1 holds at the wire, not just in JS. #}
